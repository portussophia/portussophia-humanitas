PortusHumanitas‚ĄĘ is the public reading and provenance surface for a bounded humanistic corpus: literary formations, philosophical bridges, bilingual phrases, early operational translations, corrections, and the records that preserve their integrity.

It does not convert lived experience into proof. It does not ask biography to validate research. It makes the human and intellectual continuity of the inquiry inspectable without treating intimacy as a public entitlement.

## Public corpus

This site may publish:

- sealed artifacts already authorized for public standing;
- byte-identical public copies of those artifacts;
- their existing ledger, witness, hash, and seal records;
- editorial introductions that state chronology, relation, and present standing;
- documented corrections and supersession relations.

## Private source estate

The complete source estate remains under the private `PortusSophia/Book` tree. Private letters, health information, intimate correspondence, unfinished material, and internal records do not become public merely because they contributed to the history of the inquiry.

## Relation to PortusSophia

PortusHumanitas is not a competing doctrine or a decorative origin story. It preserves the point at which humanistic inquiry reached an unresolved bridge and disciplined research became necessary.

The main [PortusSophia public interface](https://public.portussophia.com) presents the field architecture. Humanitas preserves the lived, literary, and philosophical provenance through which that architecture became possible.

The PortusHumanitas phrase register will preserve bilingual formulations as provenance-bearing textual objects.

Each public entry should identify:

- the exact Latin and English wording;
- the source artifact and path;
- the date or corpus stage;
- its present standing or disposition;
- its relation to later PortusSophia terminology;
- any correction, supersession, or translation note.

## Editorial rule

A phrase may be memorable without being canonical. It may be historically important without remaining present doctrine. It may anticipate later vocabulary without being equivalent to it.

The register therefore does not present inspirational quotation tiles. It preserves the relation between wording, context, transformation, and standing.

Public entries will be added only from material already authorized for publication.

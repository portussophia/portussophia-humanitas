*Phrases encountered through source, relation, and present disposition—not detached from the inquiry that carried them.*

The PortusHumanitas phrase register preserves selected formulations as provenance-bearing textual objects. These entries are editorial excerpts, not sealed artifacts. Their inclusion makes a developmental relation visible without promoting every memorable line into present doctrine.

## Editorial rule

A phrase may be historically important without remaining canonical. It may anticipate later vocabulary without being equivalent to it. Literary, theological, ethical, and proto-formal language must retain the register in which it was written.

## Register boundary

The register does not prove a continuous theory hidden inside the earlier writing. It records phrases that carried a pressure, distinction, or orientation before later technical vocabulary existed. Corrections and differences remain part of the record.

*The records that may establish an artifact’s identity, chronology, and byte integrity without turning technical witness into philosophical proof.*

PortusHumanitas does not create a second release authority. When a public artifact is admitted, this site may present the provenance records already attached to it and explain their scope. At present, no sealed artifact or associated provenance record has been admitted.

## What witness may establish

A ledger entry, witness statement, hash, freeze record, or seal may establish that:

- a particular artifact existed in a particular form;
- its bytes match a declared digest;
- a named process or witness recorded a specific act and scope;
- a later public copy remains identical to the referenced object;
- a correction or supersession relation was documented rather than silently substituted.

## What witness does not establish

These records do not independently establish:

- philosophical truth or scientific correctness;
- originality or priority beyond the scope of the dated record;
- external endorsement or academic peer review;
- present canonical authority;
- permission to publish private or restricted material;
- the validity of every claim contained in the witnessed file.

A checksum can show that bytes did not change. It cannot show that the text is true.

## Admission path

The public provenance chain begins only after release authority is established:

1. identify the authorized public artifact;
2. preserve the authoritative title, version, date, and standing;
3. copy the artifact without normalization;
4. register its declared digest and provenance references;
5. run the repository verifier;
6. publish the routing entry and bounded editorial orientation;
7. preserve later corrections beside the historical artifact.
